# Code of Conduct

## Our Pledge

We, the contributors and maintainers of **PolyCode-Frontend** at **QuantumLogicLabs**, pledge to make participation in our project and community a harassment-free experience for everyone — regardless of age, body size, disability, ethnicity, sex characteristics, gender identity and expression, level of experience, education, socioeconomic status, nationality, personal appearance, race, religion, or sexual identity and orientation.

We are committed to fostering an open, welcoming, diverse, inclusive, and healthy community.

---

## Our Standards

### ✅ Expected Behavior

Participants in this project are expected to:

- **Be respectful and inclusive** — Use welcoming and inclusive language. Treat all people with dignity.
- **Collaborate constructively** — Offer and receive feedback with professionalism and good intent. Critique ideas, not people.
- **Assume good faith** — Approach ambiguous situations charitably. Most misunderstandings are not malicious.
- **Communicate clearly** — Write precise, readable commit messages, PR descriptions, and issue reports.
- **Acknowledge contributions** — Credit others' work appropriately in comments, changelogs, and documentation.
- **Respect differing opinions** — Healthy disagreement is welcome; hostility is not.
- **Help newcomers** — Welcome developers new to the project or to frontend development broadly.
- **Stay on topic** — Keep discussions focused on the technical and collaborative work at hand.

### ❌ Unacceptable Behavior

The following behaviors are prohibited in all project spaces:

- Harassment, intimidation, or discrimination of any kind.
- Sexualized language, imagery, or unwanted sexual attention.
- Trolling, insulting or derogatory comments, and personal attacks.
- Publishing private information (e.g., home address, email) without explicit permission — *doxxing*.
- Deliberate misgendering or use of rejected names.
- Sustained disruption of discussions, issues, pull requests, or community calls.
- Any conduct that would be considered inappropriate in a professional setting.

---

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies within all project spaces, including:

- The GitHub repository (issues, pull requests, code reviews, discussions, wikis)
- Official communication channels (Slack, Discord, email threads, etc.)
- Public events or conference representation on behalf of QuantumLogicLabs or PolyCode-Frontend

It also applies when an individual is officially representing the project in public spaces.

---

## Enforcement

### Reporting

If you experience or witness unacceptable behavior, please report it by contacting the maintainers at:

**📧 conduct@quantumlogicslabs.io**

Reports will be handled with discretion, confidentiality, and care. You will receive acknowledgment within **48 hours** and a resolution update within **7 days**.

When reporting, please include:
- A description of what occurred
- When and where it happened
- Any supporting context or evidence (links, screenshots, etc.)
- Your preferred level of involvement in the follow-up

### Enforcement Process

The maintainers are obligated to clarify acceptable standards and will take appropriate, proportionate action in response to any reported violations. The enforcement ladder is as follows:

| Level | Violation Type | Consequence |
|-------|----------------|-------------|
| **1 — Correction** | Minor, unintentional, or first-time violation | Private written warning with explanation |
| **2 — Warning** | Repeated or more serious violation | Formal warning; temporary restriction from interaction |
| **3 — Temporary Ban** | Sustained or serious unacceptable behavior | Temporary ban from all project spaces |
| **4 — Permanent Ban** | Pattern of harassment, threats, or egregious conduct | Permanent ban from all project spaces |

Maintainers who do not follow or enforce this Code of Conduct in good faith may face consequences as determined by other members of the project leadership.

---

## Conflict Resolution

Before escalating to the reporting process, contributors are encouraged to resolve minor disagreements directly and respectfully. If a direct conversation is not comfortable or appropriate, maintainers can be asked to mediate.
